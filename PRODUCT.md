# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Everyday people watching their own money for scams and suspicious activity. They open Noscam in a moment of doubt — a charge, a message, a request — and need a clear read before they act.

## Product Purpose

Noscam helps people decide whether something looks like a scam **before** they act on it. Success means they rarely miss a real scam, even when that means occasional false alarms.

## Positioning

A clear “is this a scam?” check for everyday people — not a full finance dashboard.

## Brand Personality

**Calm, clear, protective.** Quiet confidence over urgency. The interface should feel like a steady second opinion: plain language, obvious next steps, no drama. Borrow the quiet hierarchy and restraint of tools like Linear and Notion — clear structure, no visual noise.

## Anti-references

- Loud “cybersecurity” / red-alert threat theater (shields, hacker aesthetics, panic UI)
- Bloated personal-finance dashboards (charts everywhere, budgets, net-worth theater)
- Generic SaaS marketing (purple gradients, feature-card grids, startup hype)

## Design Principles

1. **One job.** Every screen answers “is this a scam?” — not “how’s my money?”
2. **Catch over comfort.** Prefer surfacing real risk, even at the cost of false alarms; never bury a warning to feel polished.
3. **Calm protection.** Protective without panic; urgency lives in the content of a finding, not in chrome or motion.
4. **Show the call, not the cockpit.** Sparse hierarchy, plain language, obvious next action — no dashboard theater.
5. **Trust through clarity.** People should understand what to do next without decoding jargon or threat aesthetics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA as the baseline: keyboard access, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives, and body/placeholder contrast that meets 4.5:1 (large text 3:1).
